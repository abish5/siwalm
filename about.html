About Us

SIWALM is a non-profit training, research and sustainable development institute with headquarter in Ottawa, Canada and operations centre in Mogadishu, Somalia. The institute is committed to help Somalia develop its water and land resources for sustainable food security, and rural and urban development.

The institute was founded in 2018 by Somali-Canadian research team with the conviction that capacity building and technology transfer are essential for sustainable development of natural resources. We also believe that it might take longer the country to achieve the Sustainable Development Goals (MDGs) and related targets by 2030 without scaling up local capacity development at individual, leadership, institutional and societal level through education, training and knowledge dissemination.

SIWALM aims are (i) to build, enhance and strengthen local human capacities for irrigation development, integrated water management and climate change adaptation and (ii) to facilitate the transfer and dissemination of efficient irrigation systems and innovations to local universities, research centers, NGOs, public and private sector organizations, extension services and farmers through collaboration, partnership and networking.
