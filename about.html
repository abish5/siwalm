OUR WORK

SIWALM is a non-profit training, research and sustainable development institute with headquarter in Ottawa, Canada and operations centre in Mogadishu, Somalia. The institute is committed to help Somalia sustainably develop its water and land resources for food, nutrition, livelihood and environmental security.

The institute was founded by Somali-Canadian research team in 2018 with the conviction that capacity building and technology transfer are essential for sustainable development of natural resources. We also believe that it might take longer the country to achieve the Millennium Development Goals (MDGs) target without scaling up local capacity development at individual, leadership, institutional and societal level through education, training and knowledge dissemination.

SIWALM aims are to build, enhance and strengthen local human capacities for irrigation development, integrated watershed management and climate change adaptation, and to facilitate the transfer and dissemination of modern irrigation technology and innovations to local universities, research centers, NGOs, public and private sector organizations, extension services and farmers through collaboration, partnership and networking.

SIWALM vision is to become a World class Centre of Excellence in Water and Land Management Training, Research and Extension Services for Sustainable Agriculture and Rural Development.

SIWALM’s mission is to contribute to the education and training of professionals, extension workers and farmers and to build the capacity of institutions active in the field of water and land management in Somalia.

SIWALM's objectives are to:

  • Train undergraduate students, young professionals and policy makers in integrated water management principles and practices, efficient agriculture water management strategies and modern irrigation technologies through workshops, short-term and long-term courses

  • Train farmers and extension workers in sustainable agriculture practices, improved irrigation technologies and enterprise skills through farmer field schools

  • Carry out basic and applied research for developing strategies for efficient management of on-farm water resources to enhance agricultural productivity and ecosystems sustainability

  • Promote and implement Integrated River Basin Management (IRBM) approach in the river basins of Somalia

  • Provide consultancy, extension and advisory services in irrigation development and water management activities

SIWALM work is guided by our five core values and incorporates three cross-cutting themes of gender mainstreaming, environmental sustainability and good governance.

  • Teamwork: We work to common goals based on open and honest communication

  • Innovation and Creativity: We commit to strive for continuous improvements through innovation and promoting a learning organizational culture

  • Accountability and Transparency: We keep partners well informed on all decisions and issues and make accessible all programs and projects records to our stakeholders

  • Sustainability: We build in sustainability from the start of programs and projects to promote good governance, technical and financial capacity

  • Empowerment and Leadership: We will use every opportunity to empower local community both men and women including youth to make their lives better

SIWALM will pursue meaningful and productive partnerships and team work with a wide variety of organizations that represent academia, international development, private sector, government line agencies, NGOs and local community so as to enable synergies that have a direct result on finding innovative solutions to water management and agriculture sector problems in Somalia. Some of our current potential partners include Somali National University, McGill University and International Development Research Center (IDRC).
